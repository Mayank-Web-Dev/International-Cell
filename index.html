About Us

“Where Global Problems Meet Global Solution”

The International Cell at the Department of Psychology, Christ (Deemed to be University), Delhi NCR, fosters global connections and enhances the academic and cultural environment for psychology students. We create international collaborations and networking opportunities, integrating cutting-edge research with practical applications. Our focus on cultural competence and global perspectives prepares students to become leaders and innovators in psychology.

Mission

• Enhance academic rigor

• Work towards inclusive leadership

• Promote cultural diversity on campus

• Advance cultural sensitivity in psychological practices

• Develop cross-cultural competency workshops

• Make a global impact on individuals and our campus

Vision

• Organize guest talks by renowned experts in psychology

• Implement an immersion program to experience psychological practices in diverse settings

• Invite international psychology scholars in residence

• Promote cross-cultural understanding

Humanistic Psychodrama

A certificate course on Humanistic Psychodrama was conducted for students in the Department of Psychology, featuring Dr. Hans-Werner Gessmann as the resource person. Dr. Gessmann, a professor, founder of Humanistic Psychodrama, and Director of the International Centre for Clinical Psychology and Psychotherapy, led the session. Approximately 40 undergraduate students attended, engaging in discussions on the concepts and processes related to Humanistic Psychodrama. Each session included a brief question-and-answer segment, allowing students to interact directly with Dr. Gessmann. Overall, the course provided a valuable and interactive experience on this emerging area of psychology.

Intercultural Education

Dr. Marta Milani recently visited Christ (Deemed to be University) to deliver a guest talk on her expertise in intercultural education. She is a Senior (Tenure Track) Assistant Professor in the Department of Human Sciences at the University of Verona, where she obtained her PhD in Education and Lifelong Learning in 2014. Dr. Milani coordinates the Center for Intercultural Studies, led by Prof. Agostino Portera, and is a member of the Cooperative Learning Study-Research-Training Group. Her research focuses on developing intercultural competence in educational settings and using Cooperative Learning to promote inclusion. Her work is aligned with the department's research themes of citizenship and social inclusion, migration and multicultural societies, and group cooperation and conflict.

Dr. Nikita Jain

Dr. Nikita Jain, from Christ (Deemed to be University), Delhi NCR, presented a paper titled "Ontogenesis of Reflexive Praxis in University Classrooms in India: A Qualitative Study" at the Joint Conference by the Association for Psychosocial Studies (APS) and the Association for Psychoanalysis of Culture and Society (APCS). The conference was held on 17-18 June at St. Mary’s University, Twickenham, UK. Her study delved into the development of reflexive practices within Indian university classrooms, offering qualitative insights into educational dynamics and methodologies.
